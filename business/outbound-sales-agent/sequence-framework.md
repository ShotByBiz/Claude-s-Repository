# Outreach Sequence Framework

Cadence is a frame, not a script — every touch is regenerated for the specific
prospect. Stop the moment they reply or opt out.

| # | Day | Angle | Ask |
|---|-----|-------|-----|
| 1 | 0 | Trigger + their problem | 15-min call |
| 2 | 3 | One concrete outcome / mini case relevant to them | reply "worth a look?" |
| 3 | 7 | Different angle on the same pain (reframe, don't repeat) | call |
| 4 | 12 | Short, useful resource (no ask fatigue) | soft |
| 5 | 18 | Breakup — "should I close your file?" | yes/no |

## Rules that matter more than the cadence
- **Relevance > volume.** 20 hand-researched accounts beat 2,000 blasts.
- **Warm the domain** before sending: dedicated domain, SPF/DKIM/DMARC, gradual
  volume ramp. Deliverability is the product; copy is the garnish.
- **One ask per message.** Mixed asks tank reply rates.
- **Branch on reply, never on a fixed timer once they engage.**
- **Measure reply rate and booked rate, not opens** (open tracking is unreliable
  and hurts deliverability).

## Metrics to log per campaign
sent → reply% → positive% → booked → qualified → closed. Optimize the worst
step. Typical healthy cold B2B: 30–50% open (untracked-estimate), 5–12% reply,
1–3% booked from cold. Beating that comes from targeting, not clever subject lines.
