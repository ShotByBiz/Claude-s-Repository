# Objection Handling

The 8 you'll actually hear, with responses. Keep them short and bridge back to
the live demo.

**1. "We already have a receptionist / answering service."**
> Perfect — this isn't a replacement, it's the net under them. It catches the
> calls that hit voicemail: lunch, after hours, when they're on the other line.
> Your team keeps the calls they can take; we catch the ones they can't.

**2. "It sounds robotic / customers hate AI."**
> Fair concern — call the demo and judge for yourself. Most people can't tell,
> and it always discloses it's a virtual assistant if asked. A natural AI that
> answers beats a human voicemail that doesn't.

**3. "Too expensive."**
> Let's check the math on your numbers. [Run roi_calculator.py live.] If you're
> losing ~$Xk/mo to missed calls, $297 to recover most of it is the cheapest
> hire you'll make. And there's no contract.

**4. "I need to think about it."**
> Totally — that's why there's no contract and a 14-day guarantee. Want me to
> just turn it on for two weeks? If it doesn't catch calls you were missing, you
> pay nothing and we shut it off.

**5. "Is this even legal / what about privacy?"**
> Yes. It discloses it's an AI assistant when asked, follows call-recording laws
> for your state, and we never give medical/legal/financial advice — it books
> and takes messages. You approve exactly what it's allowed to say.

**6. "What if it says something wrong?"**
> It only speaks from a facts sheet you sign off on, and it's instructed to take
> a message rather than guess. Anything it can't handle becomes a flagged
> callback — nothing falls through.

**7. "We don't get that many calls."**
> Then it's pure peace of mind and after-hours coverage — and it's the cheap
> tier. The one emergency call it catches at 9pm pays for the year.

**8. "Send me some info."**
> I'll do better — I'll text you a demo number so you can call it like a
> customer right now. That's the info. What's the best cell to text?

## Meta-rule
Every objection bridges to **"call the demo."** The product is the closer; your
job is to get them to dial it.
