# Stream D — Done-For-You Micro-Services (deliver → get paid)

Your stated goal: *"All I want to be doing is saying here's this completed product
and receive payment,"* with sales communication mostly automated. This stream is
built for exactly that. It works best as **productized services** — fixed scope,
fixed price, fast turnaround — so there's almost no negotiation and the "close"
can be largely templated.

## What we can actually do well together
These are tasks I can produce or heavily assist with, where you do the final
human check + the account/posting/payment:

- **Video clipping & simple edits** — long video → captioned vertical clips
  (ties directly to your clipping skills).
- **Photo retouching / cleanup** — background removal, color/exposure fixes,
  blemish cleanup, e-commerce product images. (You run the editor; I write
  the workflow, batch instructions, and client comms.)
- **AI content** — blog posts, product descriptions, ad copy, social captions,
  email sequences, SEO briefs.
- **Document / data work** — formatting, data cleanup, spreadsheet automation,
  slide decks, resume/LinkedIn rewrites.
- **Prompt/automation setup** — building the agent kits in this repo for clients.

> Honest division of labor: I generate the work and the words; **you** hold the
> accounts, do the final quality pass, hit send, and collect payment. Some tasks
> (photo/video) need you to operate the actual editing app — I provide exact
> steps, not the rendered file, unless you bring the files in.

## Where the buyers are (June 2026 research)
- **Fiverr** — buyers search and order productized gigs (often <24h turnaround).
  Best fit for "here's the finished product" because *they* come to *you*.
- **Upwork / Freelancer / PeoplePerHour** — clients post jobs; you send proposals.
- **Behance** — showcase a portfolio to attract image-editing/retouching clients.
- **Demand sectors for photo work:** e-commerce, real estate, fashion, social
  media, event photography. (Product image cleanup is steady, repeatable volume.)

## The "deliver → get paid" workflow
1. **List productized offers** (see `offer-catalog.md`) on Fiverr/Etsy so buyers
   come pre-sold on a fixed scope.
2. **Auto-handle intake** — a templated intake form captures everything needed so
   there's no back-and-forth (see `sales-automation.md`).
3. **Produce** — I do the heavy lifting from the intake; you do the final check.
4. **Deliver + get paid** — through the marketplace's escrow (safest) or an
   upfront invoice for off-platform clients. Never start off-platform work
   without payment or a deposit.
5. **Ask for the review + repeat** — reviews drive more inbound, closing the loop
   toward "buyers come to me."

## How automated the "close" can really be
- **Fully templatable:** gig listings, FAQ replies, intake questions, delivery
  messages, review requests. A canned-response / auto-reply setup handles ~80%.
- **Needs you (briefly):** final quality approval, edge-case questions, and
  anything requiring judgment or account access. Marketplaces also require the
  account holder (you) for ToS reasons.
- **Payment protection:** prefer marketplace escrow or upfront/deposit invoicing.
  This is the guardrail against doing work and not getting paid.

## On me "finding leads" for you — what's realistic
I can (a) research where demand is, (b) give you repeatable search queries to find
live public job posts, and (c) write every message you send. I **cannot** ethically
auto-scrape platforms behind their ToS, mass-DM strangers, or close deals as you —
that risks bans and breaks platform rules. The scalable, durable version is
**productized listings where buyers come to you**, fed by your clip-account
audience. See `sales-automation.md` for the find-and-pitch method and templates.
